About Me

Alright, enough formal talk, lets move onto something more fun! Now sadly I cannot talk about my old dnd campaign since I lost track of my notebook since the great upheaval (when I moved into my college dorm) so my life story will have to do.

I come from a low income family with my mother coming from the Dominican Republic, and my father from Salvador. They met in the Big Apple (also known as New York) and thats where they had me! But before I was born, they decided that Vermont would be a better place to raise a family and moved there, and its within these green hills that I was born and raised, along with my two younger siblings. While here, I was set into Kingstreet Youth Center during my middle school years since my parents were divorced and my mother was normally to busy at work to watch over my siblings and I. While at this center I found my interest in readings!... through a reading contest for an ipod as the prize. Although I was only interested in reading so that I can play games with an IPod, I continued reading as I became invested in the stories and learned just how entertaining good storytelling can be. Through my interests in games and stories, I became more interested in video games that told stories and hoped to develop some so that I may also start creating my own stories, but in order for me to do so I felt that I needed to understand how to first create a video game. My parents had me join in some activities to help jumpstart my learning such as in Lego Camp where we learned to build not just Legos but also software using Scratch. While working on Scratch, I found the process of making the game to be fun and enjoyed my time there. Next, they had me join the Governor's Institutes of Vermont for one of their programs that had me working with wires, coding, and how to work with a Raspberry Pi. Although I was not able to develop games, I still found myself enjoying the process of designing and creating software and seeing it function made it felt like magic to me. So when I took my first computer science class in Milton High School, one could say that I was one of the more enthusiastic students of the class and what I had learned had only heightened my excitement and solidified my desire to pursue a career in computer science. I find that software development allows me to tinker with my creations in a way that I cannot replicate anywhere else, and allows my projects to come to life.
